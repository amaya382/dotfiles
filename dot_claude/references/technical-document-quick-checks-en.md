# Quick Checks: English-Specific

Companion checklist for `technical-document-en.md`. Run through `technical-document-quick-checks.md` (language-independent, same directory) first, then check the following. See the corresponding section of the rule file for details.

## Voice and stance

- [ ] Passive voice with a real actor rewritten to active (§1)?
- [ ] Reader not addressed as "you" in argumentative passages (§1)?
- [ ] Established field terms used, no near-synonym substitution (§1)?

## Sentence structure

- [ ] No pseudo-cleft Wh- openers ("What makes this hard is...") (§2)?
- [ ] No paragraph opening with "So." or sentence opening with "Look," (§2)?
- [ ] No three consecutive sentences of similar length (§2)?
- [ ] Paragraph length, tone, and endings varied (§2)?

## AI Slop (English-specific)

- [ ] No throat-clearing openers, emphasis crutches, meta-commentary, performative emphasis, vague declaratives, or business jargon (§3-1)?
- [ ] Filler adverbs and intensifiers not scattered across the document (§3-2)?
- [ ] Decorative metaphors ("journey", "landscape", "unlock", "harness") replaced with plain verbs (§3-3)?
- [ ] No em dashes and no filler discourse-marker paragraph openers ("So.", "Look,", "Now,") (§3-4)? Dramatic fragmentation and other cross-language structural tells belong to the language-independent checks (§7-§8) above.
- [ ] English hedging vocabulary used per §3-5 ("reportedly", "appears to", "seems to" for unverified; "might/may/could" reserved for genuine uncertainty; mid-temperature evaluations mixed in)? The general hedging rule was already checked in the language-independent list (§5, §9).

## Formatting and punctuation

- [ ] Code and logs in code blocks, asides in footnotes (§4)?
- [ ] Bold at definition, quotation marks on re-reference (§4)?
- [ ] Headings are single natural phrases without periods or two-part separators (§4)?
- [ ] No stray `**` markers or decorative emoji (§4)?
